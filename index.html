Calculus in Food Security: Optimizing Broiler Harvest

Interactive Bioscience Dashboard for SFM Majors | Context: Malaysian Poultry Sector

The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysia, poultry is a primary protein source. Ensuring continuous and efficient broiler chicken supply is vital for national Food Security.

Farmers need to know exactly when a chicken reaches its peak growth rate to optimize feed efficiency. Feeding a chicken past its peak growth wastes resources, while harvesting too early yields less meat.

* Data modeled using logistic growth parameters representative of tropical Cobb 500 broilers, aligning with guidelines from the Department of Veterinary Services (DVS) Malaysia and the Cobb-Vantress Broiler Performance Guide.

The Mathematical Model

Weight Function (Logistic Growth):

Calculates total chicken weight \( f(x) \) in grams on day \( x \).

\[ f(x) = \frac{3000}{1 + 65e^{-0.15x}} \]

Derivative Function (Rate of Change):

Calculates the Daily Weight Gain \( f'(x) \) in grams/day.

\[ f'(x) = \frac{29250e^{-0.15x}}{(1 + 65e^{-0.15x})^2} \]
